Famous Places in Bihar and Nearby

1. Bodh Gaya

Distance from Patna: 110 km
Why Famous: Lord Buddha attained enlightenment here under the Bodhi Tree.
Key Attractions: Mahabodhi Temple (UNESCO Site), Bodhi Tree, Thai Monastery
Famous Food: Tibetan momos, thukpa, khaja
Best Time to Visit: October to March
Things to Do: Meditation, monastery visits, photography

2. Nalanda

Distance from Patna: 95 km
Why Famous: Ancient Nalanda University, a center of learning.
Key Attractions: University ruins, Archaeological Museum, Xuanzang Memorial Hall
Famous Food: Litti Chokha, sattu drinks, jalebi
Best Time to Visit: October to March
Things to Do: Explore ruins, museum visit

3. Rajgir

Distance from Patna: 100 km
Why Famous: Ancient capital of Magadh Empire, associated with Buddha & Mahavira.
Key Attractions: Venuvana, Peace Pagoda, Ropeway, Hot Springs
Famous Food: Sattu paratha, lassi, kachori
Best Time to Visit: October to March
Things to Do: Ropeway, bathing in hot springs, heritage walk

4. Patna

Distance from Patna: Central
Why Famous: Historic city (ancient Pataliputra)
Key Attractions: Golghar, Patna Sahib Gurudwara, Bihar Museum
Famous Food: Litti Chokha, malpua, makhana kheer
Best Time to Visit: October to February
Things to Do: River walk, museum visit, local shopping

5. Vaishali

Distance from Patna: 55 km
Why Famous: Birthplace of Mahavira, world’s first republic
Key Attractions: Ashokan Pillar, Buddha Stupa
Famous Food: Khichdi, sattu dishes
Best Time to Visit: November to February
Things to Do: Explore ancient sites

6. Vikramshila

Distance from Patna: 270 km
Why Famous: Ancient Buddhist University
Key Attractions: Vikramshila Mahavihara ruins
Famous Food: Bhagalpuri sweets, ghugni
Best Time to Visit: November to March
Things to Do: Historical exploration

7. Kesaria

Distance from Patna: 110 km
Why Famous: Tallest Buddhist Stupa in India
Key Attractions: Kesaria Stupa
Famous Food: Local sweets, litti
Best Time to Visit: November to February
Things to Do: Visit stupa, take photos

8. Pawapuri

Distance from Patna: 90 km
Why Famous: Nirvana place of Mahavira
Key Attractions: Jal Mandir, Samosharan Mandir
Famous Food: Jain food
Best Time to Visit: October to March
Things to Do: Religious pilgrimage

9. Madhubani

Distance from Patna: 170 km
Why Famous: Madhubani/Mithila painting
Key Attractions: Villages like Jitwarpur
Famous Food: Mithila thali, dhuska
Best Time to Visit: October to March
Things to Do: Buy paintings, attend workshops

10. Bhagalpur

Distance from Patna: 235 km
Why Famous: Silk City
Key Attractions: Vikramshila, silk factories
Famous Food: Bhagalpuri sweets, fish curry
Best Time to Visit: October to March
Things to Do: Buy silk, river views

11. Sitamarhi

Distance from Patna: 140 km
Why Famous: Birthplace of Sita
Key Attractions: Janki Temple
Famous Food: Sattu drink, khichdi
Best Time to Visit: November to February
Things to Do: Religious tourism

12. Valmiki National Park

Distance from Patna: 275 km
Why Famous: Bihar’s only tiger reserve
Key Attractions: Jungle safari, tiger spotting
Famous Food: Local tribal dishes
Best Time to Visit: October to March
Things to Do: Safari, nature walks

13. Muzaffarpur

Distance from Patna: 75 km
Why Famous: Litchi farming
Key Attractions: Litchi gardens, local parks
Famous Food: Shahi litchi, chaat
Best Time to Visit: June (for litchi), October to February
Things to Do: Taste litchi, visit fairs

14. Tomb of Sher Shah Suri (Sasaram)

Distance from Patna: 150 km
Why Famous: Beautiful mausoleum
Key Attractions: Tomb in lake
Famous Food: Mughlai food, kebabs
Best Time to Visit: November to March
Things to Do: Visit historical site

15. Jal Mandir (Pawapuri)

Distance from Patna: 90 km
Why Famous: Mahavira’s nirvana
Key Attractions: Temple in lotus pond
Famous Food: Jain cuisine
Best Time to Visit: October to March
Things to Do: Meditation, peaceful walk

16. Navlakha Palace (Rajnagar)

Distance from Patna: 170 km
Why Famous: Ruined royal palace
Key Attractions: Heritage architecture
Famous Food: Mithila cuisine
Best Time to Visit: October to February
Things to Do: Explore ruins, photography

17. Mundeshwari Temple

Distance from Patna: 205 km
Why Famous: One of the oldest temples
Key Attractions: Stone temple, hilltop view
Famous Food: Sattu, local sweets
Best Time to Visit: October to February
Things to Do: Pilgrimage, trekking

18. Lauriya Nandangarh

Distance from Patna: 270 km
Why Famous: Ashokan pillar & stupas
Key Attractions: Buddhist archaeological site
Famous Food: Traditional dishes
Best Time to Visit: October to February
Things to Do: Historic exploration

19. Jim Corbett National Park (Uttarakhand)

Distance from Patna: 950 km
Why Famous: First national park of India
Key Attractions: Safari, tigers, Ramganga River
Famous Food: Kumaoni dishes
Best Time to Visit: November to June
Things to Do: Safari, camping, bird watching
