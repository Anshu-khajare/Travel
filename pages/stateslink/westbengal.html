Famous Places in West Bengal

1. Kolkata

Distance from Patna: Approximately 600 km
Why Famous: Cultural capital of India, known for its colonial architecture, art galleries, and cultural festivals.
Key Attractions: Victoria Memorial, Howrah Bridge, Dakshineswar Kali Temple, Indian Museum, Park Street, Princep Ghat, Rabindra Sarobar, Hooghly Riverfront.
Famous Food: Rosogolla, Mishti Doi, Kathi Rolls, Fish Curry, Puchka (Pani Puri).
Best Time to Visit: October to March.
Things to Do: Explore historical sites, enjoy local cuisine, attend cultural festivals, evening strolls along the Hooghly Riverfront.

2. Darjeeling

Distance from Patna: Approximately 475 km
Why Famous: Renowned hill station known for its tea plantations and panoramic views of the Himalayas.
Key Attractions: Tiger Hill, Batasia Loop, Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, Peace Pagoda, Rock Garden, Sandakphu.
Famous Food: Momos, Thukpa, Darjeeling Tea.
Best Time to Visit: March to May and October to November.
Things to Do: Ride the toy train, visit tea estates, trekking, enjoy mountain views.

3. Sundarbans

Distance from Patna: Approximately 700 km
Why Famous: Largest mangrove forest in the world and home to the Royal Bengal Tiger.
Key Attractions: Sajnekhali Bird Sanctuary, Tin Kona Island, Dobanki Watch Tower.
Famous Food: Freshwater fish dishes, local Bengali cuisine.
Best Time to Visit: November to February.
Things to Do: Boat safaris, bird watching, explore mangrove forests.

4. Siliguri

Distance from Patna: Approximately 450 km
Why Famous: Gateway to the Northeast and the hill stations of Darjeeling and Sikkim.
Key Attractions: Bengal Safari, ISKCON Temple, Kali Mandir, City Centre.
Famous Food: Bengali sweets, momos, local street food.
Best Time to Visit: October to February.
Things to Do: Shopping, visit temples, explore nearby hill stations.

5. Mirik

Distance from Patna: Approximately 500 km
Why Famous: Serene hill station known for its lake and tea gardens.
Key Attractions: Sumendu Lake, Bokar Monastery, Simana View Point, Tingling View Point.
Famous Food: Momos, Thukpa, local Tibetan cuisine.
Best Time to Visit: March to May and September to November.
Things to Do: Boating, nature walks, visit tea gardens.

6. Digha

Distance from Patna: Approximately 700 km
Why Famous: Popular seaside resort town.
Key Attractions: Digha Beach, Marine Aquarium, Amarabati Park, Shankarpur Beach.
Famous Food: Seafood, Bengali sweets.
Best Time to Visit: October to February.
Things to Do: Beach activities, visit aquarium, enjoy seafood.

7. Dooars

Distance from Patna: Approximately 600 km
Why Famous: Gateway to Bhutan, known for its forests and wildlife sanctuaries.
Key Attractions: Gorumara National Park, Buxa Tiger Reserve, Samsing, Lataguri.
Famous Food: Local tribal cuisine, Bengali dishes.
Best Time to Visit: October to March.
Things to Do: Wildlife safaris, trekking, visit tea gardens.

8. Kalimpong

Distance from Patna: Approximately 500 km
Why Famous: Hill station known for its colonial architecture and monasteries.
Key Attractions: Deolo Hill, Durpin Dara Hill, Dr. Graham's Homes, Zang Dhok Palri Phodang Monastery.
Famous Food: Momos, Thukpa, local Tibetan dishes.
Best Time to Visit: March to May and October to December.
Things to Do: Sightseeing, visit monasteries, enjoy panoramic views.

9. Murshidabad

Distance from Patna: Approximately 400 km
Why Famous: Historical city with rich Nawabi heritage.
Key Attractions: Hazarduari Palace, Katra Masjid, Kathgola Gardens, Nizamat Imambara.
Famous Food: Mughlai cuisine, Bengali sweets.
Best Time to Visit: October to March.
Things to Do: Explore historical monuments, visit museums.

10. Kurseong

Distance from Patna: Approximately 475 km
Why Famous: Known as the 'Land of White Orchids', a quiet hill station.
Key Attractions: Eagle's Crag, Dow Hill Park, Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose Museum, Forest Museum.
Famous Food: Momos, local hill cuisine.
Best Time to Visit: March to June.
Things to Do: Nature walks, visit tea estates, explore museums.
